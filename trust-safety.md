# Trust & Safety

## What it is

This page explains the trust assumptions and safety model of SSS.

## Why it matters

Users need to understand how SSS differs from centralized exchanges and AMM-based systems.

## How it works

SSS is designed to provide:
- On-chain verifiability
- Clear execution logic
- Transparent system behavior

At the same time, users should understand that public test systems may still evolve.

## Summary

- Verifiability matters
- Trust boundaries should be explicit
- Public test implies evolving product behavior
